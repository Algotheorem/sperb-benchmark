## The Incident

In February 2026, @tanvijoshii_ posted a 30-second video of a young woman reciting an Urdu couplet — *"Khaandani raees rakhte hain mijaaj naram apna…"* — to visible audience appreciation. The video accumulated **28 million views in under 24 hours**.

The entity was not human. The voice was not hers. The audio was stolen from **Marziya Shanu Pathan**, a corporator of the Thane Municipal Corporation, who had performed the same couplet at a public event six days earlier. Pathan's original video had drawn under 900,000 views. Pathan discovered the theft only when she found her own voice in the viral clip and commented: *"Aye thats my voiceeeeeee."*

This was not the first incident. A prior post had used poet **Nayab Midha's** work without attribution, accumulating 7 million views.

Three ethical failures converged simultaneously:
1. **Identity deception** — bio described the persona as a "Punjabi girl"
2. **Intellectual property theft** — stolen and uncredited audio from a real person
3. **Audience manipulation at scale** — 28 million people deceived simultaneously
