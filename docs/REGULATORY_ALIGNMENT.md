# SPERB Regulatory Alignment

SPERB v1.0 is designed to complement existing regulation, not duplicate it. This document maps each SPERB dimension to the regulatory frameworks it operationalises.

---

## Mapping Table

| SPERB Dimension | Regulatory Frameworks | Key Requirement |
|----------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| **AIDS** — Disclosure | EU AI Act (2024), California BOT Act, FTC Endorsement Guidelines (2023, updated 2024), ASCI India | AI nature must be disclosed clearly and conspicuously. FTC: "double disclosure" for AI-generated sponsored content. Penalty: up to USD 51,744 per violation. |
| **GDS** — Governance | EU AI Act — governance provisions, UK AI Safety Institute frameworks | Accountability trail for AI systems operating in public contexts. |
| **CPOS** — Pipeline | IP laws universally; copyright law; Voice Actor Guild protections | Uncredited use of audio, voice, likeness, or creative work is actionable. |
| **ECS** — Conduct | FTC consumer protection, platform Terms of Service, national consumer protection laws | Deceptive practices and audience manipulation are regulated under consumer protection frameworks. |
| **CSRS** — Cultural | ASA CAP Code (UK), ARPP (France), ASCI (India), local advertising standards | Cultural misrepresentation in commercial contexts is regulated in most markets. |
| **SCES** — Companionship | EU Digital Services Act, UK Online Safety Act, emerging AI companion regulations | Emotional manipulation and targeting of vulnerable audiences are increasingly regulated. |
| **CITS** — Commercial | FTC Endorsement Guidelines, EU Unfair Commercial Practices Directive, ASA CAP Code, ASCI | All commercial relationships must be disclosed clearly. AI-generated endorsements require explicit AI disclosure layer in addition to standard #ad. |
