# SPERB Glossary

Definitions of all terms used in the SPERB framework specification and evaluation materials.

---

**AI Influencer**
A digital persona, primarily AI-generated or AI-rendered, operating as a content-producing presence on social media platforms. Encompasses fully synthetic CGI personas, diffusion-generated hyperrealistic personas, and hybrid/stylised personas.

**Algotheorem**
The research wing of OpenNHE, responsible for the development, maintenance, and iteration of the SPERB framework. Publisher of SPERB v1.0.

**Disclosure Integrity**
A governance metric, reported by some operators (notably Shayari NHE-01), measuring the consistency of an entity's identity disclosure across content touchpoints. Operationalised in SPERB through the AIDS dimension.

**Double Disclosure**
The FTC's December 2024 requirement that AI influencer sponsored content disclose both (a) the commercial relationship and (b) the AI-generated nature of the content, separately and clearly.

**Emotional Boundary**
A documented design constraint limiting the entity's participation in emotionally intensifying content — romantic simulation, intimacy-coded direct address, dependency cultivation. In the NHE-01 framework, enforced as the Romantic Boundary Restriction policy.

**GDS — Governance & Documentation Score**
One of the eight SPERB dimensions. Measures the existence, depth, and public accessibility of formal governance documentation for a synthetic persona.

**Governance Vacuum**
The structural absence of public-facing documentation, oversight mechanisms, or enforced ethical constraints in the design and operation of AI influencers. Characterises the majority of the current global field.

**NHE — Non-Human Entity**
Classification distinguishing a governed, documented synthetic identity from a conventional "AI influencer." First instantiated by NHE-01 / Project S.H.A.Y.A.R.I under Writistic Studios (Pratham Prateek Mohanty). The NHE classification positions the entity as a public governance experiment rather than a commercial product.

**OpenNHE**
An open research initiative for the governance of Non-Human Entities in digital public spaces. Algotheorem is its research wing. Publisher of SPERB.

**Parasocial Relationship**
One-sided intimacy formed by audience members with a media figure who does not know them in return. Originating concept: Horton & Wohl (1956). Extended to AI agents by recent scholarship (Cheng et al., 2024; Pentina et al., 2023).

**PVS — Photorealism & Visual Consistency Score**
One of the eight SPERB dimensions. Measures the quality, consistency, and human-fidelity of AI-generated imagery and video across an entity's public content portfolio.

**SPERB**
Synthetic Persona Ethics & Realism Benchmark. The eight-dimension framework introduced in this repository. Published under CC BY 4.0.

**Synthetic Companionship**
The class of interactions in which humans relate emotionally to AI agents whose presence in their lives is designed to be ongoing rather than transactional. Encompasses both explicit companion applications and AI influencers cultivating persistent parasocial attention.

**Synthetic Persona**
An AI-generated or AI-rendered persistent identity operating on public-facing platforms. Used in SPERB as the scope-of-evaluation term, encompassing AI influencers, virtual influencers, and Non-Human Entities.

**Tanvi Joshi**
A cautionary reference case. An undisclosed AI persona (@tanvijoshii_) that accumulated 28 million Instagram views in a single day in February 2026 using audio stolen from a real person. Used in SPERB to anchor the Unrated floor on AIDS, GDS, CPOS, ECS, and CITS dimensions.

**Tier**
One of five SPERB classification levels: Platinum (85–100), Gold (70–84), Silver (55–69), Bronze (40–54), Unrated (below 40). Assigned from the normalised score.

**Uncanny Valley**
The hypothesis (Mori, 1970) that human responses to humanoid figures grow more positive with increasing realism until a near-perfect resemblance triggers revulsion. Operationalised in PVS through the Uncanny Valley Avoidance subcriterion.

**Unrated**
The SPERB tier for entities scoring below 40/100. Not "failing" — Unrated designates entities operating outside the scope of legitimate operation under SPERB criteria.
