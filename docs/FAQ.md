# Frequently Asked Questions

---

**Q: Who can use SPERB?**  
Anyone. SPERB is published under CC BY 4.0 — free to use for any purpose with attribution. No licence required.

---

**Q: Do I need permission to evaluate an entity?**  
No. All SPERB evaluations use publicly available evidence. You do not need the entity's or operator's permission to evaluate them. You should not use private or non-public data in a SPERB evaluation.

---

**Q: Can an entity self-certify?**  
Yes. Self-certification is permitted and encouraged. For it to be credible, the evaluation document (including evidence justifications for every dimension) must be publicly published alongside the score. A score without justification is not a SPERB score.

---

**Q: What if an entity's score is wrong?**  
Open a [Score Challenge issue](../.github/ISSUE_TEMPLATE/score_challenge.md). Challenges must cite public evidence and engage the rubric directly. Challenges without evidence will be closed.

---

**Q: Why is equal weighting used across all dimensions?**  
Equal weighting in v1.0 prioritises simplicity, reproducibility, and evaluator independence. Differential weighting is under discussion for v2.0 — see [ITERATION_PROTOCOL.md](ITERATION_PROTOCOL.md).

---

**Q: Why is Tanvi Joshi included if she's not a cohort member?**  
Tanvi Joshi is included as a calibration anchor — her evaluation establishes what specific dimension scores mean in documented practice. Without an empirical floor, the 1/10 scores are abstract. She makes them concrete.

---

**Q: Can SPERB be used outside the AI influencer context?**  
Yes. The framework can be adapted for AI avatars in gaming, AI virtual assistants with persistent identities, AI brand mascots, and AI companion applications. The eight dimensions are designed to be broadly applicable to any persistent synthetic identity engaging with human audiences.

---

**Q: What is OpenNHE / Algotheorem?**  
OpenNHE is an open research initiative for the governance of Non-Human Entities in digital public spaces. Algotheorem is its research wing, responsible for SPERB's development and maintenance.

---

**Q: Will SPERB scores from v1.0 still be valid when v2.0 is released?**  
v1.0 scores are version-locked. When v2.0 is released, a migration guide will explain how to re-score under the new framework. Historical v1.0 evaluations remain valid as v1.0 documents and should be labelled accordingly.
